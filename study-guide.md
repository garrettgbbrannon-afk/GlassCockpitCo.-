## Regulations

### Medical Certificate Duration (FAR 61.23)
- **Age 40+ at time of exam:** third-class medical valid to the **last day of the 24th month** after
- **Under 40 at time of exam:** valid to the **last day of the 60th month** (5 years)
- Always the last day of the month, never the exact anniversary date
- Example: 51-year-old, exam May 3 → valid to **May 31, two years later**

### NTSB Substantial Damage (Part 830)
- Reportable if damage **adversely affects structural strength, performance, or flight characteristics**
- **Not reportable:** engine failure damage alone, landing gear damage alone, single prop/rotor blade damage, bent fairings/cowling
- Immediate notification required for accidents (death, serious injury, or substantial damage)
- Written report within **10 days** for accidents

### Annual Inspection (FAR 91.409)
- Required within the **preceding 12 calendar months**
- Extends to the **last day of the 12th month**, not the anniversary date
- Example: inspection July 12 this year → due no later than **July 31, next year**

### VFR Cruising Altitudes (FAR 91.159)
Above 3,000 ft AGL, below 18,000 ft MSL:
- **Magnetic course 000°–179°:** odd thousands + 500 ft (3,500 / 5,500 / 7,500…)
- **Magnetic course 180°–359°:** even thousands + 500 ft (4,500 / 6,500 / 8,500…)
- Example: course 185° → **4,500 ft**

### Required Aircraft Documents (ARROW)
- **A**irworthiness certificate
- **R**egistration
- **R**adio license (international only)
- **O**perating limitations (POH)
- **W**eight & balance data

### VFR Fuel Reserves (91.151)
- **Day:** enough to reach destination + **30 min** at normal cruise
- **Night:** enough to reach destination + **45 min** at normal cruise

### Alcohol Rules (91.17)
- No alcohol within **8 hours** of flight ("bottle to throttle")
- BAC must be under **0.04**

### Right of Way (91.113)
- **Distress aircraft** always has right of way over everyone
- **Least maneuverable first:** balloon → glider → airship → airplane/rotorcraft
- **Converging same category, same altitude:** aircraft on the **right** has right of way
- **Head-on:** both alter course **to the right**
- **Overtaking:** overtaken aircraft has right of way; pass to the right

### Minimum Safe Altitudes (91.119)
- **Congested area:** 1,000 ft above the highest obstacle within a **2,000 ft horizontal radius**
- **Other than congested:** 500 ft above the surface (or 500 ft from any person, vessel, structure)

### Oxygen Requirements (91.211)
- **12,500–14,000 MSL:** crew oxygen required after 30 minutes at those altitudes
- **Above 14,000 MSL:** crew oxygen required at all times
- **Above 15,000 MSL:** oxygen must be provided to passengers

### BasicMed Limits (AC 68-1A)
- Max **250 KIAS**
- Max altitude **18,000 ft MSL**
- Max **6 occupants**, **6,000 lbs** aircraft
- Not for compensation/hire

### Currency Requirements
- **Flight review:** every **24 calendar months**
- **Passenger currency:** 3 takeoffs and landings in preceding **90 days**
- **Night passenger currency:** 3 takeoffs and landings **to a full stop at night** in preceding 90 days

### ADS-B Out Requirements
Required in:
- Class A, B, and C airspace
- Above 10,000 ft MSL (except below 2,500 AGL)
- Within 30 NM of Class B primary airport (Mode C veil equivalent)

## Airspace

### Class A
- **18,000 MSL to FL600**, everywhere in the US
- Not depicted on sectional (VFR not permitted)
- IFR only, altimeter set to **29.92**
- Requires: instrument rating, IFR flight plan, ATC clearance

### Class B
- Around major hub airports; multi-tier "upside-down wedding cake"
- **Solid blue thick lines** on sectional
- Requires **explicit ATC clearance** ("cleared into Bravo")
- VFR mins: **3 SM, clear of clouds**

### Class C
- Airports with tower + radar approach control
- Two-tier: 5 NM core to surface + 10 NM shelf from ~1,200 to 4,000 AGL
- **Solid magenta thin lines** on sectional
- Requires **two-way radio contact established** + Mode C transponder
- VFR mins: **3 SM, 500 below / 1,000 above / 2,000 horizontal**

### Class D
- Towered airports without radar approach
- Single cylinder, ~4-5 NM radius, up to ~2,500 ft AGL
- **Dashed blue single ring** on sectional
- Requires **two-way radio contact established**
- VFR mins: **3 SM, 500 / 1,000 / 2,000**

### Class E
- "Everything else controlled" — fills the gaps
- Floor varies:
  - **Fuzzy magenta boundary:** Class E to the surface
  - **Fuzzy blue boundary:** Class E starts at 700 ft AGL
  - **Default:** 1,200 ft AGL (implied, not usually drawn)
- Ceiling: up to 17,999 ft MSL
- No clearance or radio contact needed for VFR
- VFR mins **below 10,000 MSL:** 3 SM, 500/1,000/2,000
- VFR mins **above 10,000 MSL:** 5 SM, 1,000/1,000/1 SM horizontal

### Class G
- Uncontrolled airspace
- No radio or clearance needed
- **Day, 1,200 AGL or less:** 1 SM, clear of clouds (loosest mins)
- **Day, above 1,200 AGL (below 10,000 MSL):** 1 SM, 500/1,000/2,000
- **Night, at any altitude below 10,000 MSL:** 3 SM, 500/1,000/2,000
- **Above 10,000 MSL:** 5 SM, 1,000/1,000/1 SM

### Standard VFR Cloud Clearance
**"5-10-20" — 500 below / 1,000 above / 2,000 horizontal** (with 3 SM visibility)
- Applies almost everywhere below 10,000 MSL

### Special VFR
- Requires ATC clearance, 1 SM visibility, clear of clouds
- **At night:** requires instrument-rated pilot AND IFR-capable aircraft

### Other Airspace Types
- **MOA (Military Operations Area):** magenta hashed border. Not restricted, exercise caution
- **Restricted (R-XXXX):** blue hashed border. Requires authorization
- **Prohibited (P-XXXX):** blue hashed. Never enter (White House, Camp David)
- **Alert (A-XXXX):** high volume of training or unusual activity

## Weather

### Air Stability
- **Stable air:** resists vertical motion → stratiform clouds, steady precip, poor visibility, smooth
- **Unstable air:** rises freely once displaced → cumuliform clouds, showery precip, turbulence, good visibility away from storms

### Front Types
- **Warm front:** slow, shallow slope → stable, stratiform, steady rain, long duration, smooth
- **Cold front:** fast, steep slope → unstable, cumuliform, showery/heavy, short duration, rough
- **Occluded:** combination of both, complex weather
- **Stationary:** not moving, weather sits and lingers

### Cloud Types
- **Low clouds** (surface–6,500 ft): stratus, cumulus, stratocumulus
- **Middle clouds** (6,500–20,000 ft): altostratus, altocumulus
- **High clouds** (above 20,000 ft): cirrus, cirrostratus, cirrocumulus
- **Vertically developed:** cumulonimbus, towering cumulus (span the whole troposphere)

### Precipitation Pairings
- **Steady precip:** stratiform clouds, stable air, little/no turbulence
- **Showery precip:** cumuliform clouds, unstable air, turbulence

### Thunderstorm Stages
1. **Cumulus stage:** updrafts only, cloud building
2. **Mature stage:** updrafts AND downdrafts, **most dangerous** — heaviest rain, hail, lightning, microbursts
3. **Dissipating stage:** downdrafts dominate, rain tapers off

### Icing
- **Clear ice:** large water droplets (freezing rain, cumulus) — heavy, hard to remove, more dangerous
- **Rime ice:** small droplets (stratus) — rough, opaque, more brittle
- **Carburetor icing:** most likely in humid, low-power conditions; can occur above freezing OAT

### Fog Types
- **Radiation fog:** clear, calm nights from ground cooling; burns off after sunrise
- **Advection fog:** warm moist air over cold surface (coastal)
- **Upslope fog:** moist air pushed up rising terrain

### Weather Briefings
- **Standard:** comprehensive, default
- **Abbreviated:** update to a previous briefing, or specific info
- **Outlook:** requested when proposed departure is **6+ hours** away

### Weather Advisories
- **AIRMET Sierra:** IFR conditions, mountain obscuration
- **AIRMET Tango:** moderate turbulence, sustained winds, wind shear
- **AIRMET Zulu:** moderate icing, freezing levels
- **SIGMET:** severe conditions hazardous to all aircraft (icing, turbulence, dust storms, volcanic ash)
- **Convective SIGMET:** thunderstorms, tornadoes, hail ≥3/4 inch

### Density Altitude
Increased by: **high temperature, high elevation, high humidity** ("high, hot, humid")
- Higher DA = worse performance (longer takeoff roll, reduced climb, less lift, less engine power)
- Standard: 29.92 in Hg, 15°C at sea level

### Altimeter Errors
- **Warm air:** atmosphere expands → indicated altitude reads LOWER than true (you're actually higher)
- **Cold air:** atmosphere compresses → indicated altitude reads HIGHER than true (you're actually lower)
- **"From high to low, look out below"** — flying to lower pressure/temp, altimeter over-reads

### METAR Basics
- US METARs start with **K** (KDFW, KLAX, KJFK)
- Canada: C. Mexico: M. Alaska/Hawaii: P.

## Charts, Signs & Markings

### Runway Numbering
- Runway number × 10 = approximate **magnetic** heading
- Runway 9 = 090°, Runway 27 = 270°, Runway 36 = 360°
- Always magnetic, never true

### Airport Signs (Color Code)
| Sign | Colors | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Mandatory instruction | **Red** background, white letters | Stop — do not cross without clearance |
| Location | **Black** background, yellow letters, yellow border | "You are here" |
| Direction/Destination | **Yellow** background, black letters | Where to go |
| Information | Yellow background, black letters | Noise abatement, procedures |

**Memory:** Red = roadblock, Yellow = you can go.

### Runway Holding Position Sign
- Red rectangle with white numbers
- First number = runway going **left to right** in front of you
- Second number = runway going **right to left**

### Sectional Chart Airspace Symbols
- **Solid blue thick, multi-tier:** Class B
- **Solid magenta thin, 2-tier:** Class C
- **Dashed blue single ring:** Class D
- **Fuzzy magenta:** Class E to surface
- **Fuzzy blue:** Class E at 700 ft AGL
- **No line:** Class G

### Sectional Obstacle Symbols
- **Bold number:** MSL height (top of obstacle)
- **Number in parentheses:** AGL height

### Airport Symbol Colors
- **Blue:** towered airport
- **Magenta:** non-towered airport

### Frequency Codes
- **C in circle:** CTAF
- **CT:** Control Tower frequency
- **ATIS:** recorded weather/info broadcast
- **AWOS/ASOS:** automated weather
- **R in circle:** Radar approach available

### Pavement Markings
- **Hold-short line:** four lines (2 solid + 2 dashed)
  - Solid side = don't cross without clearance
  - Dashed side = safe to cross returning from runway
- **Displaced threshold arrows:** takeoff okay before it, landing must be at/beyond
- **Chevrons:** unusable pavement (overrun/blast pad)

## Performance & Weight/Balance

### V-Speeds
- **VX:** best angle of climb (obstacle clearance, distance-focused)
- **VY:** best rate of climb (time-focused)
- **VFE:** max flap extended speed (top of white arc)
- **VNO:** max structural cruising (top of green arc)
- **VNE:** never exceed (red radial line)
- **VS0:** stall speed dirty (bottom of white arc)
- **VS1:** stall speed clean (bottom of green arc)
- **VA:** maneuvering speed (max full-deflection speed)
- **VLO:** max landing gear operating speed
- **VLE:** max landing gear extended speed

### VX vs VY Memory
- **X marks the obstacle** — steep angle, slow speed, distance-focused
- **Y = vertical axis on a graph** — altitude over time, faster speed, shallower

### Load Factor by Bank Angle
- **30° bank:** 1.15 Gs
- **45° bank:** 1.41 Gs
- **60° bank:** 2.00 Gs (stall speed increases ~41%)

### Stall Speed
Increases with:
- Higher weight
- Higher load factor (steeper bank, abrupt maneuvers)
- Forward CG

### Fuel/Time/Distance Formula
**Fuel = (Distance ÷ Groundspeed) × Burn rate**
- Example: 75 SM ÷ 45 mph × 3 gal/hr = 5 gallons

### Landing Techniques (Confusing Pair)
- **Soft-field:** normal approach angle, carry power through flare, cushion touchdown in ground effect, hold nose off, keep weight on wings
- **Short-field:** steeper approach, precise touchdown point, power to idle, max braking, shortest stop

### Takeoff Techniques
- **Soft-field:** yoke back on roll, get airborne ASAP, level off in ground effect to accelerate before climbing
- **Short-field:** full brakes + full power, release brakes, rotate at VR, climb at VX until obstacle cleared, then VY

### Center of Gravity Effects
- **Forward CG:** more stable, higher stall speed, longer takeoff roll, reduced cruise performance
- **Aft CG:** less stable, easier to stall, harder to recover, can be unrecoverable if beyond limit

### Best Glide Speed
- Corresponds to **L/D max** — intersection of induced and parasite drag curves
- Total drag is minimum here → longest distance per unit of altitude lost

## Aerodynamics

### Four Forces of Flight
- **Lift** (opposes weight, generated by wings)
- **Weight** (gravity)
- **Thrust** (from engine/prop)
- **Drag** (aerodynamic resistance)

In steady, unaccelerated flight: **lift = weight, thrust = drag**

### Types of Drag
- **Induced drag:** byproduct of lift (wingtip vortices, downwash)
  - **Increases as airspeed decreases** (highest at low speed/high AOA)
- **Parasite drag:** everything not producing lift
  - **Increases with airspeed** (roughly with square of speed)
  - Sub-types: form drag, skin friction drag, interference drag

### Angle Definitions (Confusing Pair)
- **Angle of attack:** chord line vs. **relative wind** — changes constantly in flight
- **Angle of incidence:** chord line vs. **longitudinal axis** — fixed, built into airframe

**Memory:** Attack = Air (relative wind). Incidence = airplane (Iongitudinal axis).

### Left-Turning Tendencies (on takeoff, high power/high AOA)
1. **Torque** (Newton's 3rd law reaction to engine turning)
2. **P-factor** (descending prop blade takes bigger bite at high AOA)
3. **Spiraling slipstream** (prop wash spirals around fuselage, hits left side of vertical stabilizer)
4. **Gyroscopic precession** (mostly a tailwheel issue on takeoff)

### Ground Effect
- Occurs within about **one wingspan** of the surface
- Reduces induced drag (less wingtip vortex development)
- Aircraft can become airborne at lower-than-normal airspeed
- **Danger:** attempting to leave ground effect without sufficient airspeed can cause the airplane to settle back

### Wingtip Vortices / Wake Turbulence
- **Vortices sink 300–500 fpm** below the generating aircraft
- Strongest when the generator is **heavy, clean, and slow**
- **Landing behind larger aircraft (same runway):** stay above, land beyond their touchdown point
- **Taking off behind larger aircraft:** rotate before their rotation point, climb above their climb path
- **Vortices dissipate in about 3 minutes** in calm conditions

### Stability Axes
- **Longitudinal axis (roll):** controlled by **ailerons**
- **Lateral axis (pitch):** controlled by **elevator**
- **Vertical axis (yaw):** controlled by **rudder**

### Turn Coordinator
- Shows **rate of turn AND rate of roll** (yaw + roll)
- Standard rate turn: 3°/sec = 360° in 2 minutes
- Inclinometer ball shows coordination (slip vs skid)

### Adverse Yaw
- During roll input, the wing with the **down-going aileron** creates more induced drag
- Yaws the nose **opposite** the intended turn direction
- Countered with coordinated rudder input

### Stall
- Occurs at the **critical angle of attack**, regardless of airspeed or attitude
- Higher weight, load factor, or forward CG raises stall speed

## Aircraft Systems & Instruments

### Airspeed Indicator Color Bands
- **White arc:** flap operating range (VS0 to VFE)
- **Green arc:** normal operating range (VS1 to VNO)
- **Yellow arc:** caution range (smooth air only)
- **Red radial line:** VNE (never exceed)
- **Blue line** (twin engines only): VYSE (best single-engine climb)

### Pitot-Static System
- **Pitot pressure** → airspeed indicator
- **Static pressure** → altimeter and VSI
- **Pitot blocked, static clear:** airspeed acts like an altimeter (climbs → reads faster, descends → reads slower)
- **Static blocked:** altimeter freezes at blocked altitude; VSI reads zero; airspeed becomes inaccurate

### Vacuum vs Electric Instruments
- **Vacuum-powered:** attitude indicator, heading indicator (gyros)
- **Electric-powered:** turn coordinator, most modern glass panels

### Magneto System
- Independent of aircraft electrical system
- Two mags for redundancy (checked during runup)
- **Disconnected ground wire:** engine could keep running with ignition off
- Only way to stop: **mixture to idle cutoff**
- Always treat prop as HOT even with switch off

### Detonation vs Preignition (Confusing Pair)
- **Detonation:** unburned charge explodes instead of burning smoothly (uncontrolled)
- **Preignition:** fuel/air ignites too early due to hot spots (before spark)

### Carburetor Icing
- Most likely: **high humidity, reduced power settings** (descent/glide)
- Can occur even in above-freezing OAT
- Prevention: carb heat when appropriate

### 100LL Avgas
- Color: **blue** (low-lead)

### Instruments You Should Recognize
- **Attitude indicator:** pitch and bank
- **Heading indicator:** magnetic heading (yaw)
- **Turn coordinator:** rate of turn + rate of roll
- **Airspeed indicator:** IAS
- **Altimeter:** pressure altitude (MSL when set correctly)
- **VSI:** rate of climb/descent
- **Magnetic compass:** magnetic heading (with errors)

## Navigation

### AGL vs MSL
- **AGL (Above Ground Level):** height above terrain directly below
- **MSL (Mean Sea Level):** height above sea level; what altimeter reads
- Flying constant MSL over rising terrain → AGL shrinks

### True vs Magnetic
- **True heading:** relative to geographic (true) north
- **Magnetic heading:** corrected for **variation** (angle between true and magnetic north)
- **Compass heading:** further corrected for **deviation** (aircraft's own magnetic fields)
- **"East is least, west is best":** East variation subtracts from true; west variation adds

### Magnetic Compass Errors

**Acceleration Error (ANDS)** — East/West headings only
- **A**ccelerate → indicates a turn toward **N**orth
- **D**ecelerate → indicates a turn toward **S**outh

**Turning Error** — North/South headings only
- Turning through **north:** compass lags (initially shows opposite direction)
- Turning through **south:** compass leads (shows ahead of actual)

### VOR Basics
- Ground-based radio station broadcasting 360 radials outward
- Cockpit indicator: CDI needle + OBS knob + TO/FROM flag
- **CDI needle centered:** on the selected course
- **Deflected left/right:** course is to the left/right of the aircraft

### VOR Radial Interpretation
Radials are always measured **FROM the station outward**
- **FROM flag showing:** OBS setting = radial (they match)
- **TO flag showing:** radial = OBS setting ± 180° (reciprocal)
- Example: OBS 210°, TO flag → radial = 030°
- Example: OBS 210°, FROM flag → radial = 210°

### Pilotage vs Dead Reckoning
- **Pilotage:** navigating by visual reference to landmarks
- **Dead reckoning:** computing position from heading, speed, wind, and time
- Most VFR cross-country uses a blend of both

### True vs Indicated Airspeed
- **TAS increases relative to IAS** as density altitude increases
- IAS is what you fly by (safety limits); TAS is what you use for navigation (nav calculations)

## Ground Operations

### Taxiing Wind Corrections

**Quartering HEADWIND**
- **Aileron UP** on the upwind side
- Elevator neutral (or slightly back)
- "Climb into the wind" — keeps upwind wing from lifting

**Quartering TAILWIND**
- **Aileron DOWN** on the upwind side
- **Elevator DOWN** (yoke forward)
- "Dive away from the wind" — keeps tail from lifting

**Memory:** "Climb into a headwind, dive away from a tailwind."

### Traffic Patterns
- **Standard:** 1,000 ft AGL, **left-hand turns**
- Non-standard right traffic indicated by segmented circle
- Segmented circle with landing strip and traffic pattern indicators shows pattern direction for each runway
- Tetrahedron: separate wind direction indicator (points into wind)

### VASI (Visual Approach Slope Indicator)
- Two-bar system near runway threshold
- **Red over white:** on glide slope ("red over white, you're all right")
- **Red over red:** too low ("red over red, you're dead")
- **White over white:** too high ("white over white, you'll fly all night")

### PAPI (Precision Approach Path Indicator)
- Single row of four lights
- **4 white:** way too high
- **3 white, 1 red:** slightly high
- **2 white, 2 red:** on glide slope
- **1 white, 3 red:** slightly low
- **4 red:** way too low

### Class D VASI Rule (91.129)
Maintain altitude **at or above the glide slope** until lower altitude is needed for safe landing

### Clearing the Runway
Not clear until **entire aircraft** (including tail) is past the hold-short line

## Communications

### Light Gun Signals

| Signal | On the Ground | In Flight |
|---|---|---|
| Steady green | Cleared for takeoff | Cleared to land |
| Flashing green | Cleared to taxi | Return for landing |
| Steady red | Stop | Give way, continue circling |
| Flashing red | Taxi clear of runway | Airport unsafe, do not land |
| Flashing white | Return to starting point | Not used |
| Alternating red/green | Exercise extreme caution | Exercise extreme caution |

### Transponder Emergency Codes
- **7500:** hijacking
- **7600:** lost communications
- **7700:** general emergency
- Avoid dialing through these accidentally during routine changes

### Emergency Frequencies
- **121.5 MHz:** VHF international emergency (guard) — what pilots use
- **243.0 MHz:** UHF military emergency
- **406 MHz:** modern ELT digital transmission (satellite-monitored)

### Distress Calls
- **"MAYDAY, MAYDAY, MAYDAY"** — distress (life-threatening)
- **"PAN-PAN, PAN-PAN, PAN-PAN"** — urgency (concerning but not immediately life-threatening)

Include: call sign, position, situation, needs.

### ATC Clock Position Traffic Advisories
- **12 o'clock:** directly ahead
- **3 o'clock:** directly right
- **6 o'clock:** directly behind
- **9 o'clock:** directly left
- **2 o'clock:** front-right (~60° right of nose)
- **10 o'clock:** front-left (~60° left of nose)

### CTAF (Common Traffic Advisory Frequency)
- Used at non-towered airports (or towered airports when tower is closed)
- Pilots self-announce position and intentions
- Shown on sectional with **"C" in a circle** symbol
- Often shares frequency with UNICOM but describes different things

### Frequencies Compared
- **CTAF:** self-announce (no controller)
- **UNICOM:** private airport advisories/services
- **MULTICOM:** used at airports with no UNICOM (usually 122.9)
- **Tower/Ground/Approach:** actual ATC controllers
- **ATIS:** one-way recorded weather/info

## Emergency Procedures

### Engine Failure Priority
**Aviate → Navigate → Communicate**
1. Establish **best glide** airspeed
2. Pick a landing spot
3. Attempt restart / troubleshoot
4. Declare emergency (7700, 121.5)
5. Passenger briefing
6. Prep for landing

### Spin Recovery (PARE)
1. **P**ower — idle
2. **A**ilerons — neutral
3. **R**udder — opposite the spin
4. **E**levator — forward (break the stall)
Then neutralize rudder once rotation stops and recover from dive.

### Emergency Landing Gear (Complex Aircraft)
- **No universal rule** — depends on terrain
- Extended gear helps in rugged terrain (protects cabin)
- Retracted gear better on soft/water surfaces (prevents flipping)
- Follow AFM/POH

### In-Flight Fire
- Engine fire: mixture cutoff, fuel selector off, master off (per POH)
- Electrical fire: master off, identify and isolate

### Wake Turbulence Avoidance
- **Landing behind larger aircraft (same runway):** stay above, land beyond
- **Departing behind larger aircraft:** rotate before their rotation, climb above
- **Wait 3 minutes** for intersection departures behind larger aircraft

## Human Factors

### Hypoxia
- Insufficient oxygen to brain/tissues
- **Early symptoms:** euphoria, poor judgment, tingling, blue lips/fingertips
- Can occur at surprisingly low altitudes at night or with health factors
- Solution: descend, use oxygen

### Hyperventilation
- Rapid/deep breathing (usually anxiety) → too much CO2 exhaled
- Symptoms mimic hypoxia closely (dizziness, tingling)
- Solution: slow breathing, breathe into a bag

### Spatial Disorientation
- Vestibular system lies in reduced visibility
- **Trust the instruments** over bodily sensations in IMC
- Never fly VFR into IMC as a private pilot

### Traffic Scanning
- Systematic pause-and-scan across horizon
- Eye only detects motion during pauses (~1 second per 10° segment)

### Alcohol and Fatigue
- Alcohol effects magnified by altitude and fatigue
- 8-hour bottle-to-throttle rule is a minimum, not a target
- Under 0.04 BAC

### IMSAFE Checklist (self-assessment)
- **I**llness
- **M**edication
- **S**tress
- **A**lcohol
- **F**atigue
- **E**motion / Eating

### Carbon Monoxide Poisoning
- Usually from exhaust leaks through cabin heat system
- Symptoms: headache, drowsiness, dizziness
- Solution: turn off heat, fresh air, land

## Commonly Confused Pairs

Watch for these on the test — designed to trip you up.

### Detonation vs Preignition
- **Detonation:** unburned charge explodes
- **Preignition:** mixture ignited early by hot spots

### Angle of Attack vs Angle of Incidence
- **Attack:** chord vs. relative wind (dynamic)
- **Incidence:** chord vs. longitudinal axis (fixed)

### Stratiform vs Cumuliform
- **Stratiform:** steady rain, smooth, stable air
- **Cumuliform:** showery rain, turbulent, unstable air

### Warm Front vs Cold Front
- **Warm:** slow, stable, stratiform, steady rain, long duration
- **Cold:** fast, unstable, cumuliform, showery, short/rough

### VX vs VY
- **VX:** angle (steep, distance-focused, obstacle clearance)
- **VY:** rate (shallower, time-focused, normal climb)

### Soft-Field vs Short-Field Landing
- **Soft-field:** normal approach, power carried through touchdown, cushion onto surface
- **Short-field:** steeper approach, power off, precision touchdown, max braking

### AGL vs MSL
- **AGL:** height above ground (changes with terrain)
- **MSL:** height above sea level (what altimeter reads)

### True vs Magnetic
- **True:** geographic north reference
- **Magnetic:** what compass/nav uses (corrected for variation)

### Variation vs Deviation
- **Variation:** difference between true and magnetic north (geographic)
- **Deviation:** compass error caused by aircraft (per-airplane)

### ANDS vs Turning Error
- **ANDS:** speed change on E/W heading
- **Turning error:** actual turn from N/S heading

### AIRMET vs SIGMET
- **AIRMET:** hazard to light aircraft
- **SIGMET:** hazard to ALL aircraft, more severe

### Class B vs Class C Entry
- **Class B:** explicit clearance ("cleared into Bravo")
- **Class C:** two-way radio contact established (call sign acknowledged)

### CTAF vs UNICOM
- **CTAF:** the purpose (self-announce)
- **UNICOM:** the service (airport advisories)
- Often the same frequency, but describe different things

## Memory Hooks Quick Reference

- **ARROW** — required aircraft documents
- **ANDS** — Accelerate North, Decelerate South (compass error, E/W only)
- **PARE** — spin recovery (Power, Ailerons, Rudder, Elevator)
- **IMSAFE** — pilot self-assessment
- **5-10-20** — standard cloud clearance (500 below, 1,000 above, 2,000 horizontal)
- **"Red over white, you're all right"** — VASI on glide slope
- **"Red over red, you're dead"** — VASI too low
- **"White over white, you'll fly all night"** — VASI too high
- **"From high to low, look out below"** — altimeter over-reads
- **"East is least, west is best"** — magnetic variation
- **"High, hot, and humid"** — density altitude increases
- **"Bottle to throttle: 8 hours"** — alcohol rule
- **"Climb into a headwind, dive away from a tailwind"** — taxi wind corrections
- **"Attack = Air (relative wind)"** — angle of attack definition
- **"X marks the obstacle"** — VX for clearing obstacles
- **"Y-axis is vertical, gets you up fastest"** — VY for best rate of climb
